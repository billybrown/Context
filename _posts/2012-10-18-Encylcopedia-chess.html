The following is the first edition of the unreviewed and incomplete compilation of Marcel Dzama’s film, A Game of Chess. The fourteen minute video depicts a game of chess brought to life through ballet. The full video is shown below and then the directory itself immediately thereafter.

Person with Multiple Faces

A person with multiple faces is an individual who has more than one face on the same head. Usually male, this person is often depicted as a god, villain or fantastical being of ambiguous morality. Each face sometimes represents a different personality of the same individual.

In Dzama’s A Game of Chess there is a tall, black caped figure wearing a four faced mask. This character is the Black King, but is never actually seen playing on the board. As the leader of the black side, Dzama’s quad-face is perpetually wanting to annihilate pieces on the white side.

  • Janus, the roman god of beginnings and translations. He has one face looking toward the future and one toward the past.

  • Man-E-Faces, villain from the cartoon Masters of the Universe. He has three faces including a human, a monster and a robot.

  • Brahma, the four faced Hindu god of creation. Each head continually recites scripture.

  • The Mayor of Halloween Town from the movie Nightmare Before Christmas. He has one frowning face and one smiling face.

Chess Pieces Brought to Life

Chess pieces brought to life occurs when normal chess pieces exist as moving animate beings, no longer pieces to be physically moved by the players. While the pieces seem to have the ability to think for themselves they still act within the rules of the game. When pieces come in contact with each other they will literally kill or destroy their opponents. Life-sized chess pieces brought to life is not to be confused with people playing chess with people dressed up as chess pieces, which usually happens as a display of wealth.

In A Game of Chess the pieces exist in a psudo-alternate world. The sniper is the only character that can pass through between environments and is the only unmasked human figure in the chess world. The rules of the game are loosely followed but when pieces come in contact with each other players are literally stabbed, resulting in blood flow.

  • The Human Chess Game from Harry Potter. Life sized chess pieces are enchanted and and brought to life to guard the philosopher’s stone.

  • Battle Chess, a video game version of chess in which the chess pieces come to life and battle one another when capturing.

  • Holochess, a chess game from the movie Starwars that uses holographic representations of exotic aliens as the pieces.

  • The final battle in Tim Burton’s Alice in Wonderland shows marching black and white chess pieces.

Full Body Polkadot Spandex

Full body Polkadot spandex is a skin tight suit covered in dots. The suit completely covers the individual from head to toe including the individual’s face.

In A Game of Chess the pawns all wear full body Polkadot spandex suits, one side with black suits and white dots and the other in the reverse. The pawns all move in unison and are reminiscent of synchronized swimmers wearing swim caps. The eyes of the suits are cut out and the head are all slightly elongated, perhaps because of the dancer’s hair pulled back into buns.

  • Clowns often wear a red and yellow polka dot suit which only covers the body.

  • The Spot, a comic book supervillain in theMarvel Comics universe and appearing as an enemy of Spider-Man and Daredevil.

  • Green Zentai suit, a full body unmarked spandex suit. Popularized by Green Man in It’s Always Sunny in Philadelphia and two Vancouver Canucks fans.

  • 50s/60s polka dot dresses worn by Lucy in the show I love Lucy and Minnie Mouse.

Dance Fighting

Dance Fighting is the combination of dance and fighting. Usually dance is used as a metaphor for close combat in a ballet or musical but sometimes dancing is part of a real combatants fighting style. In both dance and close combat the contestants must read and react to their partner’s moves.

In Marcel Dzama’s A Game of Chess the chess game is brought to life as a dance performance, particularly ballet. An audience watches on as the pieces dance on the board and fight and kill each other as the game progresses.

  • Capoeira, a brazilian martial art that combines dance and music. Sometimes referred to as “Break Dance Fighting” this style was popularized by Eddy Gordo, a character from the video game Tekken.

  • Dance Dance Revolution, an arcade game where players compete on platforms and press big buttons with their feet.

  • The Nutcracker, a ballet where nutcracker’s come to life and battle rats.

  • Moshing, a style of dance where people push and shove into each other.

Female Snipers

Female snipers are markswomen skilled at shooting with great accuracy from long distances. The fast majority of military snipers are male.

In Marcel Dzama’s A Game of Chess the main character, if there is one, would be the sniper and main pawn, both played by the same actress. This character kills the white pieces on the chess board and literally shoots and kills the chess player for the white side in the “real world.”

  • Lyudmila Pavlichenko, a soviet sniper during World War II. With 309 kills, she is regarded as the most successful female sniper in history.

  • Annie Oakley, an American sharpshooter and exhibition shooter. Oakley's amazing talent and timely rise to fame led to a starring role in Buffalo Bill's Wild West show.

  • Mathilda, an apprentice hitwoman played by Natalie Portman in the movie Leon: The Professional.

  • Katniss Everdeen, a hunter from the book turned movie series “Hunger Games.”

Masked Audience

A masked audience is an audience whose faces are hidden and anonymoused. The audience then becomes a part of the performance or entertainment that they are watching. The masks are usually identical or very similar when the individual audience members are not characters in themselves.

In Dzama’s A Game of Chess the audience is seen wearing round, smiling, cherub-like masks throughout the film. They are very dramatic in their reactions to the chess game, sometimes ringing cowbells and shakers in their enthusiasm. The last scene of the film shows the masks being lifted from the audience only to reveal individuals completely covered in white sheets.

  • In the film Eyes Wide Shot, people are seen at a party all wearing masks watching unmasked entertainers.

  • Statler and Waldorf, a pair of Muppet characters who heckle the rest of the cast from their balcony seats.

  • Mystery Science Theater, a tv series that features a man and his robot sidekicks who provide a running commentary on B-list sci-fi movies.

  • Sleep No More, a site-specific play by theatre company Punchdrunk. Audience members are instructed to remain silent and masked at all times.

Thus concludes the 1st addition encyclopedia of Marcel Dzama’s A Game of Chess. Future addendums will be included in the 2nd addition to come out in the coming years.
